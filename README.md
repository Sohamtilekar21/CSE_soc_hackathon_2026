# CSE_soc_hackathon_2026

ClosetNinja

Problem
Many people own far more clothing than they actually wear. 
Forgotten items sit in wardrobes for months or years before eventually being thrown away, donated, or replaced by new purchases. 
This cycle encourages unnecessary consumerism and contributes to textile waste.

ClosetNinja helps users become more mindful of what they already own before they buy more.

User Journey

1. Build Your Digital Wardrobe

The user uploads photos of their wardrobe.

Using computer vision, ClosetNinja automatically identifies each clothing item and extracts attributes such as:

Clothing type (shirt, sweater, jeans, jacket, etc.)
Colour 
Material (where possible)
Season (summer, winter, rainy, all)
Style (Emo, Goth, Happy, NPC)

Each clothing item becomes part of the user's digital wardrobe.

2. Log Your Outfit of the Day

Every day, the user uploads a photo of their outfit.

The application identifies which wardrobe items are being worn and updates their usage history.

For example:

Blue Sweater
Worn on: 3rd, 13th and 25th June
Total wears this month: 3

Yellow Pants
Worn 6 times in June

Over time the application builds statistics about the user's clothing habits.

3. Encourage Mindful Clothing Use

Every 30, 60 and 90 days the application checks for clothing that hasn't been worn.

The user receives a notification such as:

You haven't worn your green jacket in 90 days.

The user can then choose one of three options:

--> Keep It

The application generates outfit suggestions that include the forgotten item to encourage wearing it again.

--> Donate or Sell It

The application recommends nearby charities or resale platforms where the item can find a new home.

--> Repair or Reuse It

If the item is damaged, the application provides nearby tailoring or repair services along with ideas for repurposing the clothing.

4. Think Before You Buy

Before purchasing new clothing, users can enter the item's price.

After entering their hourly wage, the application translates the cost into working time.

Example

Hourly wage: $10/hour
Item price: $90

The application displays:

This purchase costs approximately 9 hours of work.

This encourages users to think about purchases in terms of time rather than money, promoting more mindful spending.

--------------------------------------------------------
<span style="color:blue">Why It Matters</span>

ClosetNinja reduces consumerism by helping people:

Wear more of what they already own
Reduce unnecessary clothing purchases
Donate or resell forgotten clothing
Repair instead of replacing damaged items
Make more intentional purchasing decisions

Ultimately, ClosetNinja extends the lifespan of clothing and reduces textile waste.
